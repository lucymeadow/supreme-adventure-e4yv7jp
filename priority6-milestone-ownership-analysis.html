Which milestones should Priority 6 own vs. track?

Dream Team analysis of 41 milestones under Expand Internationally. Consultation date: April 21, 2026.

The question

Priority 6 has 41 non-completed milestones (18 active, 5 backlog, 18 draft). Which should the Priority team own, define, and hand off or build? Which are milestones from other teams' backlogs that we should just track? The current count is too high to manage effectively.

Core finding: the Peanut Butter Problem

All four experts independently concluded that 41 milestones is too many for active management. The Priority 6 team should own 10-15 milestones where local market knowledge is the unique input. Platform team milestones (Payments, 3PI, Content) belong on a lightweight dependency register, not the milestone map. Unassigned drafts are zombies consuming cognitive overhead.

Marty Cagan

MC
Marty Cagan
Product strategy — empowered teams, product vision, strategic context

Framework: Empowered team ownership test. For each milestone, ask: does the Priority 6 team own the outcome, or are they requesting a feature from a team that owns the outcome?

"The first thing I see is a classic feature team problem dressed up as strategy. Roughly half these milestones are requests to platform teams. Those aren't your milestones. Those are stakeholder requests you've written on behalf of other teams."

Classification principle:

  • OWN when the team defines the problem, discovers the solution, owns the outcome (country-specific experience, playbooks, measurement).
  • TRACK when another team owns the outcome and you're providing strategic context (Payments, 3PI, Content, platform Identity integrations).
"If you 'own' a milestone but the engineers building it report to a different team, respond to different OKRs, and prioritize based on their own roadmap — you don't own anything. You have a Jira ticket with your name on it. That's a feature team masquerading as ownership."

Eliyahu Goldratt

EG
Eliyahu Goldratt
Theory of Constraints — constraint identification, throughput optimization

Framework: Five Focusing Steps applied to milestone portfolio.

"What is THE constraint preventing international growth? It's focused execution in the focus countries. You can only make a brand feel local when you coordinate identity, payments, content, UX, and supply together for that country."

Diagnosis: 41 milestones in progress or planned is bad multitasking. If the team has capacity for 8 milestones of active management, then 33 milestones add zero throughput and infinite distraction.

  1. Identify the constraint: Coordinated end-to-end country experience delivery.
  2. Exploit: Only own milestones that ARE the coordinated country experience.
  3. Subordinate: Platform teams deliver on their schedule. State requirements, don't manage their milestones.
  4. Elevate: If a platform team can't deliver, that's a dependency escalation, not a milestone you own.
  5. Don't let inertia become the constraint: You created 41 milestones because the system encouraged it. The system is wrong.
"Tracking 20+ platform milestones doesn't make them go faster. It makes YOU go slower."

Andy Grove

AG
Andy Grove
Production-minded management — leverage, TRM, output orientation

Framework: Leverage analysis. High-leverage activities affect many people, occur at critical timing, or require unique knowledge. Low-leverage activities could be done by anyone or your involvement doesn't change the outcome.

"What is the output of the Priority 6 team? Every milestone needs to be evaluated on one dimension: does the Priority 6 team's attention on this milestone change the output?"

High leverage (OWN): Country-specific product decisions where you have unique local market knowledge. Playbooks and SLA that define the operating system for future countries. Dashboard that provides measurement.

Low leverage (TRACK or drop): Payments knows how to ship Google Pay. 3PI knows how to onboard partners. Your oversight adds nothing. Actually, negative leverage because every hour managing their milestone is an hour not spent on the high-leverage country work.

Recommended indicator pair:

  • Quantity: number of milestones actively managed
  • Quality: % of owned milestones with clear success criteria, assigned leads, and dates
"You're running a plant with 41 work orders and capacity for maybe 10. A plant manager doesn't track the steel supplier's internal production schedule. They place the order, set the delivery date, and escalate if it's late."

Antonio Nieto-Rodriguez

AN
Antonio Nieto-Rodriguez
Strategic portfolio management — focused organization, benefits realization

Framework: Purpose Launchpad + Focused Organization. Three tests for every milestone:

  1. Clear purpose tied to strategy? Placeholders with quality score 0 fail this test. Kill or return to owning team.
  2. Active executive sponsor? 63% of scored milestones have no assignee. No sponsor = zombie project.
  3. Benefits realization measurable? "Launch Insurance in Japan" is delivery. "Reduce Japanese traveler drop-off at checkout by X%" is benefits. Most milestones are framed as outputs, not outcomes.
"This is the Peanut Butter Problem. 41 milestones spread like a thin layer of peanut butter. Thin on everything. Thick on nothing."

Classification:

  • Strategic Investments (OWN): 10-15 milestones that directly change the business in focus countries.
  • Run-the-Business Dependencies (TRACK): Platform capabilities on a dependency register with escalation triggers.
  • Zombie Projects (PARK/KILL): Every draft with no assignee, no date, no purpose. These create the illusion of a plan.
"Focus is about saying no. Your milestone map should feel uncomfortably small. If it doesn't, you haven't said no enough."

Synthesis: Consensus & Divergence

Where all four experts agree

41 is far too many

Cagan: feature team coordination pattern. Goldratt: bad multitasking. Grove: negative leverage. Nieto-Rodriguez: Peanut Butter Problem. All agree on 10-15 owned milestones max.

Platform milestones aren't yours

[Payments], [3PI], and Content milestones should not be on your map as owned items. These teams own their outcomes. You provide strategic context and track delivery dates as dependencies.

Unassigned drafts are zombies

12 draft milestones with no assignee, no date, and no purpose consume cognitive overhead for zero return. Park or kill them.

Country experience work is your core

Milestones where the Priority 6 team makes product decisions informed by local market knowledge — those are yours. This is where all four frameworks converge on ownership.

Where the experts diverge

How much to track

Goldratt goes further: don't even track most platform dependencies. State requirements, let them manage. Grove and Nieto-Rodriguez prefer a lightweight tracking mechanism (dashboard check, dependency register).

Discovery ownership

Cagan raises a deeper question: are the country teams doing real discovery with local customers, or just translating US features? If the latter, even the "own" milestones are feature team work in disguise.

Recommended Next Steps

Action plan
  1. Restructure the Milestone Map. Move from a flat list to three sections: OWN (12 milestones with active management), TRACK (11 dependencies on a lightweight register), PARK (holding pen outside the active map).
  2. Create a dependency register. For TRACK milestones, maintain a simple table with: milestone, owning team, expected delivery date, escalation trigger, last status check date. Review monthly, not weekly.
  3. Triage drafts with sponsors. For each PARK milestone, either find a sponsor who will define purpose/requirements/date, or archive it. Set a 30-day deadline: if no sponsor picks it up, it moves to a "future considerations" backlog outside the milestone map.
  4. Validate OWN milestones. Run Cagan's empowerment test on the 12 OWN milestones: is the country team doing real discovery with local customers, or translating US features? If the latter, fix the discovery process before scaling.
  5. Set the indicator pair. Per Grove: track both the count of owned milestones (should stay 10-15) and the quality score of those milestones (target: all above 7/10 on the quality rubric).
