Structural Observations

Cancelled Big Bet: Hotel Expertise Has No Active Milestone

The paper calls out three "genuinely category-defining" bets. One — "Hotels as experts/connoisseurs" — has no active milestone after MLSTN1-2085's cancellation. This leaves the paper's vision for expert content, property comparison, and Q&A entirely unresourced. It also breaks the sole coverage for Objective 1's second customer problem.

Cross-Objective Placement (Now Moot)

"Become the Hotel Expert" sat under Objective 2 in the milestone map but solved Objective 1's second customer problem. This cross-objective dependency is now moot because the milestone has been cancelled entirely — but the underlying problem remains: who owns expert content?

Arranger Timing Risk

The paper lists 7 concrete arranger capabilities. The milestone commits only to discovery. No build milestone follows. Stakeholders reading the paper will expect delivered tools, not research findings, by end of 2027.

"Three Big Bets" Coverage

The paper calls out three "genuinely category-defining" bets:

  1. Hotels.comCash as a lifestyle currency — distributed across 4 milestones (Redesign, Vault, Partnerships, Primum) with no single owner for the unified vision.
  2. Hotel Hopping (multi-hotel) — covered by Multi-Hotel Shopping (now in-progress). Tight alignment.
  3. Hotels as experts/connoisseurs — no active milestone. MLSTN1-2085 (Become the Hotel Expert) was cancelled. This bet is unresourced.
"What We're NOT Doing" Alignment

The paper's not-doing list aligns well. No milestones invest in flights, cars, packages, enterprise TMC, or VR-first features. The Differentiate VR on HCOM milestone maintains the "hotels pure play" positioning.

Recommendations

  1. Decide on Hotel Expertise. MLSTN1-2085's cancellation leaves an entire customer problem and a "category-defining" bet with zero coverage. Either create a replacement milestone, redistribute the scope to existing milestones, or revise the paper to remove the bet.
  2. Decide on MoR 100%. Either create a milestone or explicitly assign it as scope within an existing milestone. This is the highest-severity infrastructure gap.
  3. Decide on price transparency. Same treatment. The new Mitigate Price Inconsistencies milestone (MLSTN1-2567) may partially address this — make the relationship explicit.
  4. Acknowledge the Arranger timing gap. Either adjust the paper's implied delivery timeline or plan a build milestone after discovery.
  5. Consider a go-to-market milestone or plan. GEO and AEO need product investment (structured data, schema markup, LLM-optimized content).
  6. Make "non-expiring status" explicit. Either commit in VIPa 2.0 scope or remove from the paper.

MLSTN1-5657 · backlog · Obj 3
