How to use this document

This is a starting point for joint discovery between Core Platform and the three partner brands. The three brands have aligned on six customer problems we all need to solve. We want Core Platform in the conversation as a peer team that can shape and propose solutions, not as a vendor receiving an order.

We expect Core Platform to push back, reframe, and bring their own view of which problems they're best positioned to lead on, which they want to enable as a tenant model, and which we've misframed. The six categories are evidence. The three questions below are the agenda.

The agenda — three questions to anchor the conversation

QUESTION 1

Drive or enable?

For each category, where does Core Platform see itself driving customer outcomes directly, and where does it see itself enabling brand-tenant configurability? What's owned at the platform layer versus what's a configurable surface?

QUESTION 2

Migrations or parallel work?

Which categories are coordinated cross-brand migrations that unlock all three brands at once, and which are parallel brand-level work that happens to share platform surfaces? Migrations compound. Parallel work doesn't. Sequence migrations first.

QUESTION 3

Loyalty alignment?

Three brands are pursuing divergent loyalty plays for 2026 (HCOM Rewards optimization, One Key on BEX, Vrbo OneKey rollout). Core Platform owns the loyalty surface. What's Core Platform's view on cross-brand loyalty alignment given that divergence?

Category F
Loyalty Platform Alignment
Measurable outcome

Each brand runs its differentiated loyalty play on top of shared platform capabilities. Cross-brand active member CVR vs. non-member is measurable. Earn-and-burn fluidity for members shopping across brands is measurable. Loyalty-driven NBV by brand against defined targets.

Why cross-brand. Each brand is investing significantly in loyalty for 2026 with divergent plays on top of a shared Core Platform loyalty surface. Three brands building on diverging primitives risks paying three times, confusing cross-brand members, and fragmenting the platform roadmap. This is the most explicit "what is the cross-brand strategy?" question in this document.
