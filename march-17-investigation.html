HCOM App SV CVR Decline Investigation

Analysis of Slack channel #tmp-hcom-app-svcvr-decline-mar17 — Quantifying the cross-organizational effort invested in diagnosing and resolving the Hotels.com app conversion rate decline first observed on March 17, 2026.

Executive Summary

Incident: A significant decline in Hotels.com App Shopping Visit Conversion Rate (SV CVR) was observed starting March 17, 2026, unique to HCOM and affecting both iOS and Android. The Pure SV CVR gap was measured at −2.12 percentage points between periods, with approximately 52% attributed to identified root causes. PAX estimated the daily financial impact at ~$487k/day.

A dedicated Slack channel was created on March 23, 2026 by Sophie Jones to coordinate the investigation. Over the following 31 days, the channel grew to 305 members spanning 8+ organizational groups plus an external vendor (Quantum Metric). Dozens of active contributors drove investigation across 20+ threads, with significant weekend work on 4+ weekends. The investigation involved recurring syncs, multiple parallel engineering workstreams, financial impact modeling, crash analysis, pricing deep-dives, and cross-team coordination at scale.

Key Events Timeline

March 17 — Incident Detected
Decline in HCOM App SV CVR first observed. Unique to Hotels.com; both iOS and Android affected. Investigation begins within existing teams.
March 23 — Channel Created
Sophie Jones creates #tmp-hcom-app-svcvr-decline-mar17 to centralize the investigation. First messages establish the scope: both platforms, HCOM-specific, and decline is significant enough to warrant cross-org mobilization.
March 25 — Investigation Mobilizes
Daniel De Oliveira sets up recurring syncs. Hari Nair asks about financial impact; Sandeep Abraham reports PAX estimate of ~$487k/day. Pandiyan Kannaiyan identifies increased pagelayout latency. Alessio Caradossi confirms both iOS and Android affected, points to backend.
March 26 — Experiments Under Scrutiny
Vita Bush links TnL 67201 (Top of PDP redesign) to latency increase. Sophie Jones posts summary of suspect experiments: Trip Screens Prefetch, search ranking change, P13N date pre-fills. Sebastian Ghetu’s iOS investigation finds no new crash trends. Daniel De Oliveira compiles master investigation tracker on Confluence.
March 27–31 — Hypothesis Refinement
PDEA prefetch optimization deployed (redesign component removed). CVR stabilizes at −3.58%. Trip Overview page event inflation identified as potential tracking issue. QM blank session replay bug raised. Channel settles into monitoring mode over the weekend.
April 1–9 — Deeper Dives
Funnel analysis shows PDP-to-CKO as worst-hit step. User segments examined (auth/anon, loyalty tiers, booking windows). App reviews declining. Crash rates rising on iOS. Multiple hypotheses ruled out (seasonality, spring sale, bots, outages, data quality). QM analysis requested.
April 10 — Investigation Escalation
Sophie Rabet posts comprehensive investigation summary with ruled-in/ruled-out hypotheses. Quantum Metric (external vendor) onboarded with NDA verification. Mark Adams begins structuring parallel workstreams.
April 11 — QM Crash Report
Srishti Jaju shares Quantum Metric crash analysis: +91% crash volume vs baseline, 4,859 crash sessions. Android is primary driver (77.5% of crashes). iOS v2026.11 identified as high-suspect release.
April 13 — Investigation Surge
Mark Adams launches 5+ parallel investigation threads: pricing inconsistency, Android crashes, iOS crashes, image CDN 404s, checkout TnLs. 60+ messages on a Sunday.
April 13–15 — Fixes Begin Landing
FocusedSearchEntryTransition crash fix merged (race condition since Dec 2024). Android EGDSIcon crash fix PRs submitted. Pricing inconsistency resolved as clickstream tracking bug (SHOPPING-9601).
April 15 — QM/UITextField Deep Dive
Marek Stoma publishes Claude + Firebase CLI analysis identifying Qualtrics SDK swizzle as UITextField crash contributor. Erik Krietsch submits fix PR.
April 19 — DDoS Attack
P1 incident INC7991196: DDoS attack on Cloud Gateway Proxy causes widespread booking degradation across all brands. Compounds CVR decline investigation.
April 20 — Waterfall Report
Serena Durrani shares CVR waterfall: −2.12 pts Pure SV CVR gap, ~52% captured in known impacts. Android rollback monitoring underway.
April 22 — Normalization
Harriet Burrell reports YoY normalization back to pre-Easter comp levels. Crash trends improving. Pascal Collet and Jesse Gill continue active crash investigation. Investigation continues for unexplained portion.
