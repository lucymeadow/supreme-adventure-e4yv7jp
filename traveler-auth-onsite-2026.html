02Our customer

We're building for the Savvy Trip Taker.

A customer segment that shops across both leisure and business trip types — frequent, often last-minute, and motivated by rewards and flexibility across every trip they take. Winning their business trips is how we win their leisure trips. That's the flywheel.

The traveler
Frequent, often last-minute. Shops across leisure and business personas. Assembles trips across platforms to maximize value.
Books transportation directly with airlines, rail, and rental cars — Hotels.com isn't their default yet
Self-booker who prioritizes convenience and flexibility within budget, consolidating spend to maximize status
Loyalty is the connective thread — they want rewards that work regardless of destination or trip purpose
Flexibility is the deciding factor, especially in last-minute business scenarios where plans change
Winning their business trips is how we win their leisure trips — that's the flywheel
The gap today
Hotels.com looks the same whether it's a Tuesday client trip or a weekend getaway.
No business-trip UX. No segment-aware home screen, no trip-type detection, no expense tools. Generic content regardless of trip intent.

No meaningful loyalty signal. Tier status doesn't feel tangible or clearly differentiated. The gap between Hotels.com and a hotel's own loyalty program — or Booking.com's Genius — is visible and felt.

The switch cost is one bad booking. A competitor with better rewards or a better cancellation policy captures them. The strategy is to make that switch unthinkable.

03Strategy overview

Three objectives. One connected experience.

The strategy goes deep on the Savvy Trip Taker — a segment that books both leisure and business trips and is most likely to generate outsized LTV. Each objective unlocks something different. Together they form a flywheel: win the business trip, reward it meaningfully, make it flexible enough to prefer always.

01
Be the best place to plan, book, and service business trips
TMC-grade tools in the same account as leisure — expense management, map-based booking, one-tap rebook, invoice receipts, arranger access. Plus trip-specific expertise: property Q&A, comparison tools, and context-aware content. Hotel Hopping — plan, price-optimize, and book multi-city trips in one flow — is a category-defining bet no OTA has attempted.
02
Create sticky flexibility for frequent last-minute travelers
Four layers: Biz Rates (gated to verified business customers), 3rd-Party CFAR (cancel-for-any-reason insurance at checkout), EG-Underwritten synthetic flexibility (Hotels.com bears the risk directly), and Change — automating true change without a cancel/rebook.
03
Use rewards to bridge business and leisure — the flywheel
Hotels.comCash as a full-featured currency: earn, burn, refund, gift, pool, and save. Paid premium tier restores 10-for-1 with CFAR and travel insurance bundled. H1 2027: co-brand credit card, status tier matching, benefits that never expire. Business trips fund leisure stays — that's the flywheel.

04Objective 1

Win Business Travel.

Be the simplest place to plan, book, and service business trips.

Two customer problems
Problem 1: Small-scale business travelers lack the specialized tools TMCs provide — booking, servicing, and expensing are all manual pain, with no business-specific experience.

Problem 2: Travelers don't get the expert context they need to make the right decision — hotel content is overwhelming and generic regardless of trip intent.
The bet
Hotels.com becomes the expert co-pilot. One account for self-bookers, arrangers, and multi-hotel itineraries with expense tools, map booking, one-tap rebook, and invoice receipts — plus trip-specific expertise: property Q&A, comparison tools, and context-aware "reasons to book/skip." Hotel Hopping — plan, price-optimize, and book multi-city trips in one flow — is a category no OTA owns today.
Three personas, starting with self-bookers
Self-bookers — expense tools, map booking, preferred biz card, invoice receipts
Arrangers — roster management, book/edit for others, pooled rewards, duty of care
Multi-hotel travelers — plan, price-optimize, and book multi-city trips in one flow

05Objective 2

Unmatched Flexibility.

More flexibility than anywhere — especially for last-minute bookings.

The gap
Frequent business travelers get stuck with non-refundable rates in exactly the scenarios where they need flexibility most — the 0–3 day window where plans change. The refundable alternative costs 15–30% more. Hotels.com loses the cancellation policy screen every day.
Four-layer solution
Biz Rates — flexible hotel policies gated to verified business customers.

3rd-Party CFAR — "Cancel For Any Reason" insurance bundled at checkout, turning non-refundable rates into flexible offers.

EG-Underwritten — Hotels.com bears the cancellation risk directly when resale probability is high enough to make it profitable.

Change — in scenarios where policies allow, automate true change experiences without requiring a cancel/rebook.

07Why this matters for your onsite

Authentication is the foundation every objective runs on.

Every product on this roadmap requires a reliable identity model. The 2027 vision you form this week determines whether the foundation is there to build on — or whether we build around a gap.

Objective 1 · Business Travel
Identity enables the business traveler experience.
Business traveler detection requires a reliable, stable identity — ML signals only improve with persistent logged-in context.
Single account spanning business + leisure is an identity architecture problem. One identity, two personas, shared rewards.
Arranger experience requires linked identities with permission scopes — book for, view, expense on behalf of.
Multi-hotel trip management requires one identity across a multi-leg itinerary.
Objective 2 · Flexibility
Identity is the access control layer for flexibility.
Biz Rates are gated to verified business customers — identity is the mechanism. No identity model, no rate gating.
CFAR and EG-underwritten flex require reliable customer identity for the risk model to score correctly. Wrong identity = mis-priced risk.
MoR shift and servicing require a canonical customer identity that travels through cancel/change flows without breaking.
Objective 3 · Hotels.comCash
Loyalty only compounds with a persistent identity.
Earn, pool, and gift require a persistent, portable identity that works across sessions, devices, and trip types.
Paid premium tier and co-brand card are identity-anchored entitlements. They fail without a reliable identity model at their root.
Status tier matching with partner programs depends on a unified identity that can be verified and shared with partners.
Business trip attribution to the rewards flywheel requires the same identity across work and leisure booking contexts.
The 2027 vision question: Every product described in the previous three slides needs an identity model that is reliable, portable, and extensible across trip types and personas. The decisions your team makes this week determine whether we build those products on solid ground or retrofit identity in later.
