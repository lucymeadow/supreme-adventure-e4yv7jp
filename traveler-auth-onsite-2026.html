Objective 1

Win business travel — expert tools in one account

Customer problem
Small-scale business travelers lack the bespoke tools TMCs offer. Shopping, booking, and servicing is complicated and cumbersome. Hotel content is overwhelming and generic regardless of trip intent.
Solution by 2027
Hotels.com delivers rich, trip-specific expertise that makes decisions easy. One account spans self-bookers, arrangers, and multi-hotel trips. Expert Q&A, map-based booking, one-tap rebook, and invoice-grade receipts.

Objective 2

More flexibility than anywhere — especially for last-minute bookings

Customer problem
Frequent business travelers can't find offers and policies that match their needs — especially in last-minute scenarios where non-refundable rates are the only option.
Three-layer solution
Biz Rates — flexible policies from hotels, gated to verified business customers.

3rd-Party CFAR — Hotels.com bundles "Cancel For Any Reason" insurance with non-refundable rates, creating synthetic flexibility.

EG-Underwritten — Hotels.com takes on the risk directly when cancellation likelihood is low or resale probability is high.
H2 2026 — In flight
  • Deliver for Flexibility & Stickiness — Waves 1–2; Biz Rates and CFAR Pilot live on HCOM
  • Integrate Add-On Services — InsurTech assurance products bundled at checkout
  • Provide Dedicated Service Solutions — Customer-tier-aware servicing (Crawl/Walk)
  • Save Your Way 2026 — Flexible savings mechanics launching
  • MoR Shift — 100% Merchant of Record enables direct cancel/change ownership
H1 2027 — On horizon
  • Multi-Hotel Shopping — Plan, price-optimize, and book multi-city trips in one checkout; first of its kind for an OTA
  • Flexibility & Stickiness Waves 3–4 — Price-drop cancel/rebook automation, change (not just cancel/rebook) experience
  • CFAR at Scale — Expanding synthetic flexibility across more rate types and markets
The big bet
No other hotel OTA combines Biz Rates + 3rd-party CFAR + EG underwriting in one product. This is a category-defining flexibility stack — not just features.

Objective 3

Hotels.comCash — a real currency, not a points program

Why This Matters for Your Onsite

Authentication is the foundation every objective runs on

Objective 1 — Business Travel
  • Business traveler detection requires a reliable, stable identity — ML signals only improve with logged-in context
  • Single account spanning business + leisure personas depends on a unified identity model
  • Arranger experience requires linked identities with permission scopes (book for, view, expense on behalf of)
  • Multi-hotel trip management: one identity across a multi-leg itinerary
Objective 2 — Flexibility
  • Biz Rates are gated to verified business customers — identity is the access control mechanism
  • CFAR claims and MoR ownership require reliable identity for dispute resolution and fraud prevention
  • Price-drop cancel/rebook automation only works with persistent, logged-in sessions
Objective 3 — Rewards
  • Hotels.comCash earn/burn attribution is identity-anchored at every transaction
  • Pooling requires group identity — household, team, or travel party linked to a single account
  • Paid tier enrollment, status tier matching, and partner loyalty linking all start with a strong identity layer
  • Gift and transfer flows require trust — verified sender/recipient identity
The strategic framing
Identity & Profiles is explicitly called out as a critical shared platform dependency across all three P7 objectives. The Jira roadmap runs from Q2 2026 through Q1 2027 with continuous Identity team involvement.

Business traveler identification starts now (Q2 2026). The arranger identity model starts Q2 2027. Group identity for pooling starts Q3 2027.

Every login experience decision your team makes in this onsite will cascade directly into whether these products ship on time, with the right experience, or have to be redesigned mid-flight.

Your 2027 traveler login vision isn't a dependency of this strategy. It IS this strategy's foundation.
