Theme model

Updated after the 2026-06-04 working session. The HCOM-owned differentiation is now two themes. Supplier-waiver flexibility (the former Theme B) is already captured under Rachel Nichols' waivers workstream, so it is no longer a standalone HCOM theme.

Tier 1 — HCOM brand differentiation (HCOM owns the opportunity). Theme A Differentiated and personalized service (human agent + self-service), organized around customer segments (international, last-minute, high tier, business) that drive use cases. Theme C Communication: communicate service benefits through product surfaces and CRM rather than traditional marketing, a joint product and marketing effort.

Tier 2 — Shared platform and supply (HCOM contributes requirements, maps to existing milestones). Agent effectiveness routes into Agent Assist MLP (MLSTN1-5684). Lodging conditions splits across relocation/overbooking, supplier accountability, and content-accuracy milestones. HCOM does not build parallel versions.

Every requirement from the BRD, the offsite deck, and the June SteerCo, mapped to its coverage status, the theme it belongs to, its Jira home, owner, and the next action for HCOM. The headline: the expensive foundations are already captured and in flight. The real work for HCOM is the differentiation themes (A and C) where a business case is still needed.
